## Situating the Quran's Purpose

I've had a hard time situating what the whole Quran is about. We're told not to set expectations, yet you need to set realistic expectations of what the Quran must address given its divine nature and content.

For a long time I wondered why the Quran deals so much with different groups of people—to the point that it almost seems like a social science text.

The Self-Centric Universe

However, from the perspective of the individual universe—the self-centric universe—each story must focus primarily on a single individual as the Self in that universe. All others presented in a person's story may or may not possess a Self. The Quran guides us to think of these as apparitions, projections designed and maintained by Allah and His malaika.

Characters as Stages of Spiritual Evolution

The Quran's main characters represent different stages in the spiritual evolution of mankind. However, it seems these characters refer to the same person throughout different stages of his or her evolution—spiritual evolution, of course.

How is it then that some of these characters, who should be the same person, are interacting with each other?

This is not so strange if you see the Quran as putting these different characters—these different stages of evolution personified—and pitting them against each other. The purpose is to learn what each of them thinks and how the latter stage of evolution, through what we might think of as retrocausality, pulls the less advanced version of the self toward the future. Toward the more advanced, more evolved, more spiritually evolved version of that self.
