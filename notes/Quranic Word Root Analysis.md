## Metaphorical Interpretation: The Zipper Model

As I mentioned before, this relates to a couple in the zipper model—a combination, a pair of counterparts. One is a human, and the other is an angel (or *malak*).

### Gender Roles as Functional Metaphors

However, it's not that the human is specifically masculine or feminine, and the angel correspondingly feminine or masculine. The roles can interchange based on the situation and what is actually happening.

- Sometimes a human can take the role of a teacher. In that situation, the angel becomes the feminine (receptive) and the human becomes the masculine (giving).
- The roles can switch: if the angel is now teaching the human, the angel becomes masculine and the human becomes feminine.

This is useful for interpretation. This is another tool in our box of methods of linguistic discernment—another toolbox we can use to get insights about what's going on. Is this about teaching? And so on.

### Divorce as Spiritual Separation

Here we see that we're talking about a divorce of sorts. But a divorce in the metaphorical understanding—based on the context I just presented—could mean that a pair between a human and an angel was no longer fruitful, or something happened that caused them to separate from each other.

We can't necessarily assume that it's for a negative purpose. It might be that there are stages: perhaps an angel is at a certain stage of their development, and a human is at a certain stage of their development. There may be harmonics where these angels and humans can only couple when both of them are at specific ranges of frequencies, so to speak, or phases of their development.
