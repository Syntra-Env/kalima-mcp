## Introduction: 2025 Reflections

This year has been full of conflict and an explosion of information, making it difficult to keep track of everything that's going on. It can be argued that the world has always had this many events. Perhaps. But this is not the point. I don't care whether what's happening now is worse or at a greater scale than what happened before. What I care about is that this is happening, and that perhaps we can learn from history to find solutions to the issues we're currently facing—or maybe not. I don't know what kind of 'solution' would actually work. I do not see any way to resolve this situation without the help of God.

This does not mean that faith is the only hope we have left. No. Faith, to me, is not a last resort but the first step—the framework through which I interpret my percepts.

## The Quran and Current Events

I want to say, "Let's take a look at current events from the lens of the Quran." But do I even know what's going on in the first place? That does not matter. The confusion is part of the situation.

From the NCU (Nafs-Centric Universe) perspective, the Quran dictates that the passages must be relevant to me. Or perhaps not—some passages are for the angels. Supposedly. But this is also for me. Even this narrative—that some passages in the Quran were at some point not expected to be understood by the readers but rather by the angels—is a narrative that has reached me, no doubt from those *malaikah* themselves, and ultimately from Allah. The Lord of the Realms has presented this information to me.

What for? What shall I do with it? I am working towards that, still unsure how I will make progress towards understanding the Quran, better understanding my mission, and progressing in the application of this understanding.

## Man-Made vs. Divine Sources

The Quran talks about confusion—groups of people orchestrating confusion and distortions of reality. To what end? They have allowed false sources to 'rule' over them. They have taken information from man-made sources.

I hear you say, "But most of the information you're consuming is also man-made, so what rebuke do you have to that?"

I say that my entire reality is designed by the angels, so taking information from man-made sources or from divine sources can *look* like the same thing. For example, I could be listening to you speak and either treat it as knowledge coming to me from a man, or see the ultimately divine source behind this information.

But this does not mean that I take information indiscriminately, because the same message from these two different perspectives can mean two very different things.

## Applying the NCU Perspective

If I listen to you, how can I look at it from the NCU perspective? This is a message to me from Allah and the *malaikah*. But what kind of message is it? Is it actually a mirror of myself being shown to me to expose my unpolished blindspots?

Perhaps. But how do you make use of this mirror effectively—using it to move forward and perfect yourself rather than taking it at face value as the absolute word of God?

There's a lot of nuance here that I know many readers will miss. I myself am barely grasping at it. But there's a very important distinction to be made here: the perspective of divine origin, by definition, requires sharp discernment and interpretation. It's a puzzle. It's like interpreting dreams or visions from your subconscious, and the similarities go deeper than surface level.

From the NCU perspective, it is important to trace a story from the perspective of a specific character as the *nafs* in the NCU, with the other characters as either apparitions or the *malaikah* orchestrating these apparitions.

### Multi-NCU Narratives

I'm not sure if some of these stories also include multi-NCU narratives. This is one of the major sources of confusion for me at the moment. I think this is the case, especially as you follow the stories of these characters starting from their earthling life all the way to their afterlife role as *malaikah*. In this role, they are no longer in an NCU themselves but are interacting with other souls in their own NCU.

So it may be that these NCUs are the 'meeting places' of these *malaikah* and the *anfus* (souls) in other dimensions too (*jaheem*, *hijr*, *ma bayna huma*, *hissab*, etc.). But what would this entail?

If this is not the case, then there is also some form of outside-NCU communication, which is somewhat hinted at in Surah An-Naml, where Jibreel addresses the other *malaikah*, ordering them to wait while he goes to investigate this particular NCU.

But I still have questions regarding this:

- Who were these other *malaikah*?
- What are they up to?
- If many of the characters in the Quran are actually the same 'character' from different facets or in different stages of their spiritual evolution, then how can we frame these situations?
- What is this about?

## Formal Structure of the NCU Framework

Let's summarize this formulaically:

**M** = Malaikah
**E** = Earthling
**NCU** = Nafs-Centric Universe

### Key Propositions

1. **Sets**: There is a set of *malaikah* M, a set of earthlings E, and a set of NCUs.

2. **Malaikah Communication**: *Malaikah* can communicate horizontally with other *malaikah*.

3. **Limited Awareness**: *Malaikah* are not aware of all *anfus* in *hissab*, as indicated by the scene in Surah An-Naml, where Jibreel approaches "he who is in the fire and who is around it"—specifically, the one around it, about whom Jibreel had his doubts.

4. **Collective Identity**: *Malaikah* seem to have a collective identity rather than an individual identity. The best analogy I have for this is a hive mind, or cells in your body. From your perspective, they don't really have individual identities but exist—each of them fully knowing their responsibility—in order to give rise to the seemingly unified identity that is 'you'.

5. **NCU Structure**: Earthlings live in NCUs. Each NCU has precisely 1 earthling, and all other seeming earthlings are apparitions that may or may not be connected to another earthling in a different NCU.

6. **Information Routing**: The *malaikah* are routers in some sense—they have the ability to transfer information from NCU to NCU, while earthlings don't have the ability to do this directly. But this is not a clear-cut rule, for earthlings may receive information directly from Allah that the *malaikah* did not receive, bypassing the need to go through the *malaikah*.

   This, I suspect, is exactly what the Quran refers to as "taking intermediaries between themselves and Allah." That is, treating these *malaikah* as all-knowing powers rather than the counterpart of the self, created by Allah to complete their intended creation.

## The Meaning of 'Groups' in the Quran

So, what do the 'groups' in the Quran refer to?

In some passages, this group identity refers to those who have not yet realized that there is no such thing as a real group in the NCU. They have taken comfort in their ideas by means of the confirmation and reinforcement they received from the members of their 'group' in their NCU.

For sure, this was intentional by Allah, but I don't think this is the primary purpose of creating groups. I think that in the social, collective structure, the Governance (*mulk*) is being revealed to those who have the eyes to see it.

But what does this mean? What is it that these groups are actually meant to teach us, *after* we realize that we are not meant to find comfort in our ideas simply because other members of our group hold the same ideas?

### Training for the Afterlife?

We did say that as *malaikah*, there is indeed direct inter-*nafs* communication and direct *malaikah*-earthling communication, although the form this takes is still unknown to us. So it might be that we're getting training for the afterlife, when group identity might become a real thing.
