I was reading Surat Al-Hijr yesterday, and I want to go over it today. This time I'm recording my voice to mention some of the things I thought about as I was reading it. I'll start reading and make comments along the way.

## Initial Observations

First, the word *yawaddu* reminds me of the word *wuddan*, which we have translated as "endearment." So what does this mean?

رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَو كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ

Perhaps this is what it means: those who have established intermediaries would have been in endearment had they been among those who ceded their nafs (soul).

"Dharhum yākulu wa yatemattāu wa yulhihimul āamelu fasowfe ya'alamoon"

I don't know what "dharhum" means—that's something I'll have to look up.

## The Preposition "Min"

Something is telling me to pay attention to the preposition *min* here. I'm talking about the Arabic preposition مِن (meem-noon).

The ayah says:

ما تسبقوا من أمة أجلها وما يستأخرون

The traditional translation would probably say something like: "The expiration date of a community will neither come before its time nor be delayed beyond its appointed time."

But why does it say من أمة (*min ummatin*)?

It's not really referring to a whole community, but rather a portion from a community. ما تسبق من أمة means it's a subset of *ummetin*, not necessarily the whole set itself.

Some people might interpret this to mean it's a specific type of community, or a subset of communities from among all possible communities that have existed.

Perhaps.

## Ayah 6

وَقَالُوا يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِي نُزِّلَ عَلَيْهِ الذِّكْرُ إِنَّكَ لَمَجْنُونَ

Let me repeat it to examine it more closely.

Who are these people speaking?

Looking back at:

وَمَا تُسْبِقُوا مِن أُمَّةٍ أَجَلَهَا وَمَا يَسْتَأْخِرُونَ

وَقَالُوا

We're talking about some communities, and it looks like we missed a hint.

وَمَا أَهْلَكْنَا مِن قَرْيَةٍ إِلَّا وَلَهَا كِتَابٌ مَّعْلُومٌ

We're discussing a community that was destroyed (هلكت). We know that either it was destroyed, or it's about to be destroyed, or this is a community being warned of destruction.

## Identifying the Community

We have many hints. If we want to find someone or some group of people in the Quran that this Surah is talking about, they would have to fit all of these criteria:

رُبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَو كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ

- These people are not *muslimin*
- They are referred to as *al-ladhina kafaru* (الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا)

ذَرْهُمْ يَأْكُلُوا وَيَتَمَتَّعُوا وَيُلْهِهِمُ الْأَمَلُ فَسَوْفَ يَعْلَمُونَ

- They are distracted
- The word ذَرْهُمْ needs investigation

I need to look at this word *dharhum*. I must understand it by finding all of its instances in the Quran—at least all instances of the root itself, if not the exact same form.
