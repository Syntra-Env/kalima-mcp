# Discomfort and Spiritual Transformation

> **Prerequisites:** [[NCU Framework and Interpretation]] | [[Angelic Possession]]
>
> **Related:** [[Channeling Session]] | [[The Compatiblity of NCUs]]

Most of my evil tendencies have developed from the urge to sooth or avoid a sense of discomfort whose origin is often difficult to pinpoint.

The other day, when I was thinking about the neutral sense of '3adhaab', or the punishment of separation, and how Allah is not really punishing you but simpliy reflecting your reality to you, I got a small hint about the energy required to see a reality that has been ignored habitually, and how this related to the discomfort and restlessness that I so often feel.

This is also related to patience, not as endurance throughout time but the steps it takes to reach your goal, or the end of the situation with which you are being patient. Or the set of competencies you need to acquire to meet the criteria for the reality you seek. This concept comes from the Quranic concept of 3iddat, which a set of competencies necessary to attain the state of Firdaus (avoiding the stomping).

Patience is about planning, and figuring out what is necessary to get oneself out of the present, unpleasant situation.

Discomfort is equivalent to energy expenditure. Enduring discomfort requires a lot of energy. But if you don't know why you're enduring it, then doing so becomes meaningless.

What if, then we reframed this discomfort or confusion and our response to it? The discomfort itself is the transformation, the forced seeing after you're forced yourself to ignore the truth that's being presented to you. This is similar to scientific findings that when you're studying and feel confused and as though you're not understanding or learning anyting, is when the real learning happens.

This is also related to consumption. The discomfort must be consumed. Which is why I once wrote a paper called the consumer of suffering. This 'suffering' may not be in fact what the Quran refers to suffering or punsihment, although it may be the same thing from a different perspective. This suffering is the active process of receiving and making your welatanschauung avaialble to be rearranged. You can imagine this will not feel very pleasant. Then why is it that sometimes learning can feel so pleasant and as though you're in a flow? And why does seeking that feeling may be counterproductive, as it may not be what you need if you are meant to consume the discomfort first. If, as you are feeling discomfort, you treat it as the obstable to that blissful feeling of connection and understanding, then you will override the discomfort and consume something that mimicks the bliss you seek. But you should not seek the feeling. What should you seek? No. Do not go. Stay here and observe. If that which you seek cannot be found in your present experience, then reevaluate your priorities. What do I mean? Allah knows what is good for you and you do not know what is good for you, but Allah grants the knowledge of the good. How does he grant this knowledge, and when? If you are always seeking what you want, when will you ever see what is being handed over to you? Shut up and listen.

What if you are confused? You don't know what you're receiving? You get irritated and eventually call it quits, throwing your hands up in the air and exclaiming "This doesn't work!". Why is that? Because you did not feel connection or bliss? Because reality did not meet your expectations, so you abandon reality and seek experiences to reinforce and confirm your expectations.

You sit and down and begin your practice of Qunuut, but you do not know what to do, where to start, where to go or what to seek. You feel lost, even if you feel the connection to Allah, you wonder what it is that's being communicated to you. You become restless, because you want to participate in your transformation, but the malaikah are working on your weltanschauung, perhaps without your awareness, and you must let them do their work.

This is akin to ordering a house to be built for you. But you want to take part of the building, even though you're not an architect yourself and are only getting in the way of the builders. In the end, your interventions have ruined the building, and when it falls, you throw your hands in the air and exlaim that this does not work, which is true, but which part of it does not work? The architects and masons you hired are masters of their respective crafts. You were the weakest link. Step aside. Cede your nafs. Let the pros do their work. They will consult you when they need to gather specifications from you to understand what kind of house you want built for yourself.

This is of course related to [[Angelic Possession]].

############

Discomfort has been internalized by social pressures to 'do' something rather than sitting with the divine experience. When I stay still, expressionless, engaging my mind, I am often met with ridicule or concern. 'I' feel irritated by this. But the discomfort is the cure, just like medicine is bitter. The discomfort is the resistance to your change. Whatever you are doing as you feel this discomfort is exactly what you should be doing.

But let's be more specific here, because discomfort comes in many forms, and I want to write about the other forms too, and what they mean. This one I'm talking about is a feeling of restlessness and urgency to do.

Patience is not about applying force to overcome an obstacle. No, in face, doing this is akin to continuously ramming your head against the wall. It's about figuring out the path that actually leads to the destination, usually without much resistance. But this isn't exactly right.

You might not know the destination, and this is where trust comes in. Patience is about trust. Trust that what you are seeking will be revealed. Trusting that the moment you seek something, Allah commands the entire universe to move to bring you what you want.

Patience is sitting down and holding the ground firmly.
